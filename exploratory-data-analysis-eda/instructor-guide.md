# **Module 10: Correlation and Regression Analysis — Instructor Guide**

**Module Time:** 120 minutes

---

## **What This Module is About**
This module introduces learners to correlation and regression analysis as tools for understanding relationships between variables. Learners explore patterns, quantify relationships, and interpret results responsibly using validated data.

---

## **Core Objectives**
- Explore datasets to identify trends, relationships, and outliers  
- Apply correlation analysis to quantify associations between variables  
- Use regression to model and interpret relationships  
- Combine numerical results with visual exploration  
- Communicate analytical insights clearly using markdown and plots  

---

## **Must-Hit Topics**
- Difference between correlation and regression  
- Interpreting correlation coefficients responsibly  
- Visual exploration of relationships  
- Basic regression concepts and interpretation  
- Risks of misinterpretation and overconfidence  

---

## **1. Introduction to Relationships in Data**
**(Time: ~20 minutes)**

- **Purpose:** Establish why understanding relationships between variables is central to data-driven decision-making.

- **Talking Points:**
  - What it means for variables to be related  
  - Real-world examples of misleading correlations  
  - Importance of context when interpreting relationships  
  - Difference between association and causation  

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Use intuitive, non-technical examples to introduce correlation  
  - Ask learners to brainstorm examples of spurious correlations  
  - Reinforce that numbers alone do not equal insight  

---

## **2. Correlation Analysis**
**(Time: ~30 minutes)**

- **Purpose:** Teach learners how to quantify and interpret relationships using correlation metrics.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Correlation coefficients (strength and direction)  
  - Using Pandas to compute correlations  
  - Visualising correlations with scatter plots and heatmaps  
  - Limitations of correlation analysis  

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Emphasise interpretation over computation  
  - Encourage learners to always pair numbers with visuals  
  - Highlight common pitfalls (e.g. assuming causation)  

---

## **3. Regression Analysis**
**(Time: ~30 minutes)**

- **Purpose:** Introduce regression as a tool for modelling and explaining relationships between variables.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Conceptual difference between correlation and regression  
  - Interpreting regression coefficients  
  - Understanding trends, direction, and magnitude  
  - When regression adds value beyond correlation  

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Focus on conceptual understanding, not mathematical depth  
  - Use simple examples to explain regression output  
  - Reinforce that regression models depend on data quality and assumptions  

---

## **4. Visual Exploration and Analytical Interpretation**
**(Time: ~20 minutes)**

- **Purpose:** Reinforce the role of visualisation in validating and contextualising numerical analysis.

- **Talking Points:**
  - How plots can reveal patterns or contradictions  
  - Using visuals to detect outliers and non-linear relationships  
  - Combining descriptive, visual, and numerical insights  
  - Communicating findings clearly  

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Encourage learners to explain what they *see* before what they *compute*  
  - Stress clarity and simplicity in plots  
  - Frame visuals as analytical evidence, not decoration  

---

## **Wrap-Up Reflection**
**(Time: ~2 minutes)**

- **Key Takeaways:**
  - Correlation and regression help quantify relationships but require careful interpretation  
  - Visual exploration strengthens and challenges numerical results  
  - Clear documentation improves analytical credibility and trust  

- **Next Steps:**  
  Learners move on to the lab, where they apply correlation and regression techniques to a dataset, visualise relationships, and document insights using markdown and saved plots.
