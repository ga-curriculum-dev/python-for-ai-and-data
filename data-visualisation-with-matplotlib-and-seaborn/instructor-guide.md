# Module 12 — Data Visualisation with Matplotlib and Seaborn: Instructor Guide

**Module Time:** 120 minutes

---

## What This Module is About

This module focuses on transforming statistical findings into clear, ethical, and professional visual communication using Matplotlib and Seaborn. Learners move from statistical inference to visual storytelling, learning how design choices affect interpretation and decision-making.

---

## Core Objectives

- Create and customise visualisations using Matplotlib and Seaborn  
- Apply visual design principles to improve clarity and interpretability  
- Translate statistical results into visual narratives  
- Critically evaluate charts for bias, distortion, and misinterpretation  
- Export and version visual assets for professional reporting workflows  

---

## Must-Hit Topics

- Why visualisation is essential after statistical analysis  
- Choosing the right chart for the analytical question  
- Design principles for clear and ethical visualisation  
- Differences between Matplotlib and Seaborn  
- Exporting, versioning, and critiquing visual assets  

---

## 1. From Statistical Results to Visual Communication  
**(Time: ~15 minutes)**

**Purpose:**  
Help learners understand why visualisation is a necessary analytical step, not an optional add-on.

**Talking Points:**

- Statistical outputs are rarely consumed directly by stakeholders  
- Visuals help validate, challenge, or contextualise statistical results  
- Visualisation bridges analysis and decision-making  
- Poor visuals can mislead even when analysis is correct  

**Teaching Tips:**

- Show examples of correct statistics paired with misleading charts  
- Ask learners what questions visuals can answer that tables cannot  
- Reinforce that visuals are part of analytical responsibility  

---

## 2. Visual Design Principles & Tooling (Matplotlib vs Seaborn)  
**(Time: ~30 minutes)**

**Purpose:**  
Equip learners with foundational design principles and an understanding of when to use each library.

**Talking Points:**

- Matching chart types to analytical intent  
- Core design principles: scale, colour, labels, clutter  
- Matplotlib: control, flexibility, precision  
- Seaborn: statistical defaults, aesthetics, efficiency  
- Design decisions influence interpretation  

**Teaching Tips:**

- Compare the same chart built in Matplotlib vs Seaborn  
- Encourage learners to justify *why* they chose a chart type  
- Emphasise readability over decoration  

---

## 3. Creating, Refining, and Critiquing Visualisations  
**(Time: ~35 minutes)**

**Purpose:**  
Develop learners’ ability to refine visuals and critically assess their effectiveness and ethics.

**Talking Points:**

- Iterative refinement of charts for clarity  
- Identifying misleading design choices (axes, colour, aggregation)  
- Visuals for non-technical audiences  
- Pairing visuals with written interpretation  
- Exporting and versioning figures as analytical artefacts  

**Teaching Tips:**

- Ask learners to critique a chart before improving it  
- Encourage saving figures with descriptive filenames  
- Frame critique as a professional skill, not personal judgement  

---

## Wrap-Up Reflection  
**(Time: ~2 minutes)**

**Key Takeaways:**

- Visualisation is an analytical and ethical responsibility  
- Design choices shape how results are interpreted  
- Professional workflows require exported, versioned visual assets  

**Next Steps:**  
Learners move on to the lab, where they create, refine, export, and critique visualisations using Matplotlib and Seaborn based on validated and analysed data.
