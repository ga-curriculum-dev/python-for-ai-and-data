# **Module 7 — Scientific Computing with SciPy: Instructor Guide**

**Module Time:** 120 minutes

---

## **What This Module is About**
This module introduces learners to scientific computing with SciPy, extending NumPy-based workflows to include numerical methods, optimisation, and statistical inference. Learners focus on choosing appropriate tools, interpreting results, and understanding assumptions behind scientific computations.

**Core Objectives**
- Apply SciPy for numerical computation beyond basic array operations
- Use optimisation and root-finding techniques to solve analytical problems
- Perform statistical analysis and hypothesis testing using SciPy
- Interpret numerical and statistical results responsibly and in context

**Must-Hit Topics**
- SciPy ecosystem and module structure
- Numerical computation (integration, interpolation, equation solving)
- Optimisation and root finding
- Statistical analysis and hypothesis testing
- Interpretation, assumptions, and analytical reasoning

---

## 1. **Introduction to SciPy and Scientific Computing**
**(Time: ~10 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  
  Establish SciPy’s role in the Python data ecosystem and how it extends NumPy for scientific and analytical tasks.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Difference between NumPy and SciPy
  - SciPy’s domain-based module structure (`optimize`, `stats`, `integrate`, etc.)
  - When numerical methods are needed instead of analytical solutions
  - Real-world examples where SciPy is commonly used

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Emphasise *tool choice*, not memorisation of functions
  - Reinforce that SciPy is about **methods**, not just syntax
  - Ask learners where exact solutions are unrealistic in practice

---

## 2. **Numerical Computation with SciPy**
**(Time: ~20 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  
  Show how SciPy enables numerical solutions for problems that cannot be solved analytically.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Solving systems of equations numerically
  - Numerical integration and approximation
  - Interpolation and estimating missing values
  - Precision vs approximation trade-offs

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Stress that numerical results are *approximations*
  - Encourage sanity checks on outputs
  - Ask learners what assumptions are being made by numerical methods

---

## 3. **Optimisation and Root Finding**
**(Time: ~20 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  
  Introduce optimisation as a core analytical task across data science, engineering, and economics.

- **Talking Points:**
  - What optimisation means in practical terms
  - Minimisation vs maximisation
  - Constraints and feasible solutions
  - Root finding vs optimisation problems
  - Common use cases (cost, efficiency, parameter tuning)

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Frame optimisation as “decision-making with math”
  - Encourage learners to verbalise the objective function in plain language
  - Reinforce the importance of interpreting results, not just obtaining them

---

## 4. **Statistical Analysis with SciPy**
**(Time: ~20 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  
  Transition learners from descriptive statistics to statistical inference.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Probability distributions in SciPy
  - Hypothesis testing and statistical comparisons
  - Common tests (e.g. t-tests)
  - Understanding assumptions behind statistical methods

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Emphasise assumptions (normality, independence, sample size)
  - Warn against “black box” statistical testing
  - Ask learners what conclusions *cannot* be drawn from a test

---

## 5. **Interpreting Results and Analytical Reasoning**
**(Time: ~10 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  
  Reinforce responsible interpretation as a core analytical skill.

- **Talking Points:**
  - Sensitivity of numerical results to inputs
  - Validating outputs against intuition or domain knowledge
  - When results may be misleading
  - The role of context in scientific computing

- **Teaching Tips:**
  - Encourage learners to explain results verbally
  - Ask “Does this result make sense?” frequently
  - Connect interpretation to real-world consequences

---

## **Wrap-Up Reflection**
**(Time: ~10 minutes)**

- **Key Takeaways:**
  - SciPy extends NumPy by enabling numerical, optimisation, and statistical methods
  - Scientific computing requires understanding assumptions, not just code execution
  - Interpretation is as important as computation in data-driven projects

- **Next Steps:**  
  Learners move into a hands-on lab applying SciPy methods to practical numerical and statistical problems, reinforcing both computation and analytical reasoning.
